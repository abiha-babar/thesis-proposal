# Help Guide — How to Talk About Your Thesis Proposal

> **Read this tonight. This will prepare you for every question a teacher might ask tomorrow.**

---

## First — Understand Your Topic in Simple Words

Imagine you run a small business and every day you receive 500 invoices (bills) in PDF format. You need to pull out specific information from each one — like the total amount, the date, and the vendor name.

You have two options:
- **Option A:** Send all 500 invoices to an online AI service. It reads them and sends back the data. Fast and accurate — but you pay a small fee every time, and your private documents leave your office.
- **Option B:** Download a smaller AI onto your own laptop. It reads the invoices for free, keeps everything private — but is it accurate enough? Is it fast enough?

**Your thesis answers: which option is smarter, and under what conditions?**

That's it. Everything else is just details around this core idea.

---

## What to Say When You Walk In

When a teacher asks *"What is your research about?"* — say this:

> *"I want to compare two ways of using AI for data extraction from documents. One approach uses cloud-based AI services that charge per use, and the other uses smaller AI models that run locally on a standard laptop for free. I want to measure which is more accurate, which is faster, and most importantly — at what point the local option becomes cheaper than paying for the cloud."*

Short. Clear. Confident.

## Questions Teachers Will Ask — And How to Answer Them

### ❓ "Why is this topic important?"
**Say:** *"Almost every organization today processes large amounts of documents — invoices, forms, records. As AI becomes the tool for this, the cost and privacy of that AI matters a lot. My research gives a practical, data-driven answer to help organizations make that decision."*

---

### ❓ "What exactly do you mean by 'data extraction'?"
**Say:** *"It means giving the AI a messy document — like a scanned invoice — and asking it to pull out specific fields, like the total amount or the date, and return them in a clean, structured format."*

---

### ❓ "How will you measure accuracy?"
**Say:** *"I will prepare a dataset of documents with a known correct answer for every field — like a master answer key. If the AI extracts the exact correct value, it scores a 1. If it gets it wrong, it scores a 0. This gives me a clear accuracy percentage for each AI."*

---

### ❓ "What hardware will you use for the local model?"
**Say:** *"A standard consumer laptop — the kind any student or professional already owns. This is intentional, because I want to prove that you don't need expensive servers or specialized hardware to run local AI for basic tasks."*

---

### ❓ "How will you calculate cost?"
**Say:** *"For the cloud AI, I will use the actual billing from the API — it charges per use, so the cost is easy to calculate. For the local model, I will calculate the cost of electricity and a small portion of the laptop's purchase price (hardware depreciation). Then I will compare these at different document volumes."*

---

### ❓ "What is the 'breakeven point'?"
**Say:** *"It's the exact number of documents per day at which running a local AI becomes cheaper than paying for cloud access. For example, if that number is 1,000 documents per day — any organization processing more than that should switch to local. My thesis will calculate this precisely."*

---

### ❓ "What if local AI is too slow?"
**Say:** *"That's actually one of my research findings. If local AI is slower, it is still useful for batch processing — for example, running 500 invoices overnight when speed doesn't matter. Cloud AI would then be recommended only for real-time, customer-facing tasks where instant response is critical."*

---

### ❓ "Is this an MS-level topic?"
**Say:** *"Yes — it involves a controlled experimental design, quantitative evaluation metrics, a financial cost model, and produces a decision framework. It contributes original, measurable findings to the field of AI deployment research."*

---

### ❓ "Have you looked at any research papers on this?"
**Say:** *"I am currently reviewing related literature on language model benchmarking, Total Cost of Ownership models for AI systems, and document understanding datasets. I plan to formally complete my literature review in the first phase of the thesis."*

*(This is a safe, honest answer — it shows awareness without claiming you've already read everything.)*

## Key Terms to Know (In Case They Come Up)

| Term | What It Means (Simply) |
|---|---|
| **SLM (Small Language Model)** | A smaller, lighter AI that can run on a regular laptop |
| **LLM (Large Language Model)** | A big, powerful AI that usually runs on the cloud |
| **Data Extraction** | Asking AI to find and pull out specific information from a document |
| **Latency** | How long it takes the AI to respond (in milliseconds or seconds) |
| **Accuracy** | How often the AI gets the right answer |
| **Breakeven Point** | The exact usage level where local AI becomes cheaper than cloud AI |
| **TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)** | The full cost of a system — including hardware, electricity, and maintenance |
| **JSON** | A clean, structured data format the AI will output results in |
| **API** | A way to connect to a cloud AI service over the internet |
| **Ground Truth** | The correct, verified answers used to check the AI's output |

## What Makes This a Strong Thesis (Say This If Asked)

1. **It's measurable** — every result is a number, not an opinion
2. **It's practical** — real organizations can directly use the findings
3. **It's timely** — local AI is a major trend in 2026 as privacy laws tighten
4. **It produces a decision tool** — not just results, but a framework others can use

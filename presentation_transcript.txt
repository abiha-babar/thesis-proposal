SLIDE 2 — THE PROBLEM
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Organizations process thousands of documents every day
  (invoices, forms, records, receipts)
- Copying out information by hand is slow, expensive, and
  error-prone
- AI can automate this — but which kind of AI, and at what cost?


SLIDE 3 — CURRENT SOLUTION: CLOUD AI
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Most organizations upload documents to an online AI service
  (e.g. OpenAI, Google)
- It works, but:
    • Costs money every time (pay per token)
    • Private documents leave the building — privacy risk
    • Costs grow fast at scale


SLIDE 4 — THE NEW OPTION: ON-DEVICE AI
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Small AI models that run on a regular laptop — no internet
- Managed through Ollama (free, open-source tool)
- Three local models this study will test:
    • deepseek-ocr  (DeepSeek AI)
    • gemma4:e4b    (Google DeepMind)
    • qwen3.5:4b    (Alibaba)
    • Phi-4-multimodal-instruct (Microsoft) — optional (if Ollama adds support)
- Free, private, offline
- But: are they accurate and fast enough?


SLIDE 5 — WHAT RESEARCH ALREADY TELLS US
----------------------------------------------------------------
Existing Work on Document Extraction:
  - DocVQA benchmark (Mathew et al., 2021)
  - FUNSD scanned forms (Jaume et al., 2019)
  - CORD receipts (Park et al., 2019)
  → Extracting data from real documents is genuinely hard

Existing Work on Making Models Smaller:
  - LLM.int8() (Dettmers et al., 2022)
  - GPTQ compression (Frantar et al., 2022)
  - Ollama makes local deployment easy (2024)
  → Running capable AI on a laptop is now possible

Measuring AI Accuracy Fairly:
  - ANLS* (Peer et al., 2024) — partial credit, handles JSON
  - BERTScore (Zhang et al., 2020) — checks meaning, not words
  → Better metrics exist but are not widely used in comparisons

SLIDE 7 — THE RESEARCH GAP
----------------------------------------------------------------
What is still missing:
  1. No study has compared multiple local models against cloud
     services on the same task (accuracy + speed + cost together)
  2. No study checks how results change with instruction wording
  3. No clear, reproducible cost model exists for local AI

→ This thesis fills all three gaps.

SLIDE 10 — HOW THE TEST WORKS
----------------------------------------------------------------
- 500 synthetic documents (invoices and forms, made-up data)
- Answer key prepared before any test begins
- Each document sent to each system with 3 instruction versions:
    A — Short:    "Extract vendor name, total, and date."
    B — Detailed: Full description of each field and rules
    C — JSON:     "Return only a JSON object with keys..."
- Why 3 versions? Fairness — AI results can vary by wording
- Best version per system used for final comparison


SLIDE 11 — WHAT WILL BE MEASURED
----------------------------------------------------------------
ACCURACY (ANLS*):
  - Partial credit for near-correct answers
  - Reported per instruction version, then best version used

SPEED:
  - Time from document sent → answer received (milliseconds)
  - Averaged across all 500 documents

COST PER DOCUMENT:
  - Cloud: billing records (tokens × price per million)
  - Local: electricity (powermetrics on Mac / powertop on Linux)
           + hardware cost (laptop price ÷ 3 years, per document)
  - Breakeven point: at what volume does local become cheaper?


SLIDE 12 — WHY 500 DOCUMENTS?
----------------------------------------------------------------
- Sample size is statistically justified, not a round guess
- Formula: n = (Z² × p × (1−p)) / E²
    p  = 0.80  (expected baseline accuracy)
    E  = ±0.035 (margin of error)
    Z  = 1.96  (95% confidence level)
  → n ≈ 500 documents
- Ensures a 4% accuracy difference between models is real,
  not sampling noise

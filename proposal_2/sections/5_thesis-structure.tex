\section{Overall Structure of the Proposed Thesis}
\label{sec:structure}

The final written thesis will be divided into the following chapters:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Introduction.} Establishes the problem of flat tool-use limitations in autonomous AI agents, motivates the shift toward agentic skill architectures, and states the three research questions this thesis answers. Describes the scope of the study and the structure of the remaining chapters.

    \item \textbf{Literature Review.} Surveys existing research on tool use in language model agents, the design of agentic skill systems, trajectory-based agent evaluation methodologies, and the effect of context window saturation on LLM reasoning. Identifies the empirical gap---the absence of a controlled comparison between flat tool use and progressive skill disclosure---that this thesis addresses.

    \item \textbf{Methodology.} Describes the experimental design in full detail: the two agent conditions (flat tool use and progressive disclosure), the construction of the 30-skill library across five domains, the 60-task benchmark at three difficulty levels, the three language model backends used, and the full set of outcome, trajectory, and efficiency metrics. Explains the Python-based orchestration controller and the measurement instrumentation.

    \item \textbf{Results.} Presents all collected data across both conditions and all model backends. Includes tables and figures showing task completion rates, hallucination counts, trajectory compliance scores, prompt token consumption, latency, and inference cost. Reports results separately by task difficulty level and skill library size.

    \item \textbf{Discussion.} Interprets the results in relation to each research question. Analyses the conditions under which progressive disclosure provides the greatest benefit, acknowledges the limitations of the study (such as the use of synthetic tasks and a single hardware configuration), and identifies directions for future work.

    \item \textbf{Conclusion.} Summarizes the main empirical findings and their practical implications for developers and organizations building or deploying autonomous agent systems.

    \item \textbf{References.} A complete list of all papers, frameworks, and datasets cited in the thesis, formatted in the standard academic bibliographic style of the institution.

    \item \textbf{Appendices.} Supporting material including: the complete SKILL.md file template used for all 30 skills, the full task benchmark with answer keys, the Python orchestration controller source code, raw results tables for all conditions and model backends, and the energy measurement configuration for each local model host.
\end{enumerate}

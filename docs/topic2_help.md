# Help Guide — Topic 2: Local AI vs. Paid Online AI for Data Extraction

> **Read this tonight. It will prepare you for every question a teacher might ask tomorrow.**

## First — What Is Your Research About? (In Very Simple Words)

Imagine a school that receives 500 student forms every semester. Each form has a student's name, ID number, and fee total written on it. Someone needs to take that information and enter it into the computer system. Doing it by hand takes days.

AI can read all 500 forms automatically and pull out the right information in minutes. This is called **data extraction** — teaching the AI to find and copy specific pieces of information from a document.

Right now, most organizations send their documents to a **paid online AI service**. They upload the document, the AI reads it, and sends back the information. It works well. But it costs money every time, and your private documents leave your building and go to a third-party server.

A new option now exists: **smaller AI tools that run directly on a regular laptop**. Free. Private. No internet needed. But the question is — are they accurate enough? Are they fast enough?

**Your research answers this:** Which option works better, and at what point does the free local option become cheaper than the paid online one?

---

## What to Say When a Teacher Asks "What Is Your Research About?"

Say this:

> *"I am comparing two ways of using AI to automatically read and extract information from documents. One way uses a paid online AI service. The other uses a smaller AI tool running directly on a regular laptop for free. I want to measure which one is more accurate, which one is faster, and at what number of daily documents the free local option becomes the cheaper choice."*

Short. Clear. Confident. Done.

## Questions Teachers Will Ask — With Simple Answers Ready

### ❓ "What do you mean by data extraction?"
**Say:** *"It means giving the AI a document — like an invoice or a form — and asking it to find and pull out specific pieces of information, like the total amount, the date, or the person's name, and return them in a clean, organized format."*

---

### ❓ "How will you measure accuracy?"
**Say:** *"I will prepare 500 test documents where I already know all the correct answers — like an answer key. If the AI extracts the exact correct value, it gets a score of 1. If it gets it wrong, it gets a 0. At the end, I calculate what percentage of all answers were correct. That is the accuracy score."*

---

### ❓ "What is the breakeven point you keep mentioning?"
**Say:** *"It is the exact number of documents per day at which running the local AI becomes cheaper than paying for the online AI service. For example, if that number turns out to be 800 documents per day — then any organization processing more than 800 documents daily should switch to the local option to save money."*

---

### ❓ "What hardware did you use for the local AI?"
**Say:** *"I used a standard, everyday consumer laptop — the kind that a student or office worker would already have. This is intentional, because I want to prove that you do not need expensive, specialized computer equipment to run local AI for basic data extraction tasks."*

---

### ❓ "How did you calculate the cost of the local option if it is free to use?"
**Say:** *"Even though the software itself is free, running it on a laptop uses electricity. I measured how much electricity the laptop consumed while processing the documents and multiplied that by the electricity price. I also included a small portion of the laptop's purchase price, since using it adds to its wear and tear over time. This gives a realistic total cost."*

---

### ❓ "What if the local AI is too slow for real-time use?"
**Say:** *"That is actually one of the findings the research is designed to reveal. If local AI is slower, my conclusion would be that it is better suited for batch processing — for example, processing 500 forms overnight when nobody is waiting for an instant answer. Cloud AI would then be the better choice only for real-time situations where someone needs an immediate response."*

---

### ❓ "What documents will you use for testing?"
**Say:** *"I will create 500 test documents myself — designed to look like real invoices and administrative forms, but using made-up data. This way I have a complete and verified answer key for every single document, and there are no privacy issues with using real sensitive records."*

---

### ❓ "What if the local AI is accurate but too slow to be practical?"
**Say:** *"Speed and accuracy are both measured and reported separately. If the local AI scores well on accuracy but poorly on speed, the conclusion is clear: it is suitable for overnight batch processing but not for instant, real-time applications. Both use cases are valid and the findings address both."*

---

### ❓ "Is this research good enough for a Master's thesis?"
**Say:** *"Yes. It involves designing a controlled experiment, building an automated evaluation system, collecting and analyzing real performance data, and producing a financial cost model with a breakeven analysis. All of this together produces original, measurable findings that have direct practical value — which is exactly what a Master's thesis is meant to contribute."*

---

### ❓ "Have you read any research papers on this?"
**Say:** *"Yes, I have reviewed studies on document information extraction, AI model compression, edge computing, and AI deployment cost analysis. My full literature review will be completed as the first phase of the thesis."*

## Key Words to Know (In Case They Come Up)

| Word | What It Means Simply |
|---|---|
| **Data Extraction** | Teaching AI to find and pull out specific information from a document |
| **Local AI / SLM** | A smaller AI tool downloaded onto your own laptop — runs offline, completely free |
| **Cloud AI / LLM** | A large, powerful AI service accessed over the internet — you pay each time you use it |
| **Accuracy** | How often the AI got the correct answer — measured as a percentage |
| **Latency** | How long it takes the AI to respond — measured in milliseconds or seconds |
| **Breakeven Point** | The number of daily documents at which local AI becomes cheaper than cloud AI |
| **TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)** | The full real cost of running a system — including hardware, electricity, and software |
| **JSON** | A simple, organized format the AI uses to return extracted data (like a structured list) |
| **API** | The connection used to send documents to a cloud AI service over the internet |
| **Ground Truth** | The correct, pre-verified answers used to check if the AI got it right |
| **Quantization** | The process of making a large AI model smaller so it can run on a regular laptop |
| **Batch Processing** | Processing a large number of documents all at once — usually overnight when speed is not critical |

## Why This Is a Strong Thesis — Say This If Asked

1. **The results are numbers** — accuracy is a percentage, speed is in milliseconds, cost is in rupees or dollars. Everything is measurable and clear.
2. **It solves a real problem** — organizations are actually asking this question right now and making decisions without proper data.
3. **It produces a decision tool** — not just results, but a financial formula any organization can use to calculate their own breakeven point.
4. **It is very relevant in 2026** — as data privacy laws get stricter and AI costs grow, this research becomes more valuable every month.
5. **It is complete** — accuracy, speed, and cost all in one study. That makes it feel thorough and well-designed.
